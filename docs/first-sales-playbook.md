# MerchantFix.ai — First Sales Playbook

This playbook defines how to find the first real MerchantFix.ai users before and immediately after the July 9 deployment window.

The objective is not broad marketing.

The objective is:

> Find 10 to 20 controlled Shopify merchant cases where Google Merchant Center product-data warnings are painful enough that a 29 EUR Fix Pack makes sense.

## Offer to sell

During first-sales validation, sell only one offer:

- Offer: MerchantFix Fix Pack
- Price: 29 EUR
- Customer: Shopify merchant, Shopify agency, Google Ads freelancer, Merchant Center consultant, feed consultant
- Problem: Google Merchant Center warning involving Shopify product data
- Input: exact warning text + Shopify CSV export
- Output: row-level diagnostic, structured report, guardrail status, evidence needed, and annotated CSV when available

Do not sell:

- approval guarantee;
- account recovery;
- full feed management;
- Shopify app setup;
- Google Merchant Center API integration;
- tax advice;
- legal or policy advice;
- performance guarantee.

## Ideal first customers

### Strong fit

These are the best first customers:

- Shopify merchant with active Google Merchant Center warning;
- Shopify agency managing product feeds for clients;
- Google Ads freelancer managing Shopping campaigns;
- Merchant Center consultant;
- feed consultant who receives messy Shopify product data;
- small e-commerce operator who does not understand GTIN, MPN, brand, or identifier_exists errors.

### Strong warning types

Prioritize leads mentioning:

- Missing value `[gtin]`;
- Invalid value `[gtin]`;
- Missing value `[brand]`;
- Missing value `[mpn]`;
- `identifier_exists` conflict;
- duplicate GTIN;
- duplicate item ID;
- SKU used as MPN;
- image issue;
- image not retrieved;
- price mismatch;
- availability mismatch;
- invalid product category.

### Avoid as first cases

Avoid leading with:

- full account suspension;
- broad misrepresentation cases;
- legal disputes;
- tax compliance;
- shipping compliance;
- policy appeals;
- merchants demanding guaranteed Google approval;
- merchants unwilling to export a Shopify CSV.

## Qualification questions

Ask these questions before selling or testing:

1. Are you using Shopify?
2. Is the problem inside Google Merchant Center?
3. Can you paste the exact warning text?
4. Does the warning mention GTIN, MPN, brand, identifier_exists, image, price, availability, category, or duplicate product data?
5. Can you export a Shopify product CSV?
6. Are you expecting a diagnosis, not a guaranteed Google approval?
7. Are you able to verify product facts from packaging, supplier, manufacturer, or Shopify admin if needed?

## Fit decision

### Green lead

A lead is Green when:

- Shopify is confirmed;
- Merchant Center warning is confirmed;
- warning matches supported or partially supported scope;
- exact warning text is available;
- CSV export is possible;
- customer understands no approval guarantee.

Action: sell or offer controlled Fix Pack test.

### Yellow lead

A lead is Yellow when:

- Shopify is likely but not confirmed;
- warning text is vague;
- CSV may be available;
- problem may be broader than product data.

Action: ask for the exact warning before selling.

### Red lead

A lead is Red when:

- not Shopify;
- no Merchant Center warning;
- full account suspension only;
- customer wants guaranteed approval;
- customer refuses CSV export;
- problem is legal, tax, policy appeal, or account recovery.

Action: do not sell the Fix Pack.

## Sales call structure

Use this order:

1. Confirm the store is Shopify.
2. Ask for the exact Merchant Center warning.
3. Identify the issue family.
4. Explain what MerchantFix can and cannot do.
5. Explain the 29 EUR Fix Pack.
6. Ask if they can export the Shopify CSV.
7. Repeat that Google approval is not guaranteed.
8. Send the payment link only when Vercel/Stripe is ready.
9. After purchase, ask them to upload the CSV through `/diagnostic`.
10. Review their feedback and record the case.
