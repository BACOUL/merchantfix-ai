# MerchantFix.ai SEO Sniper Strategy

This document defines the SEO strategy for MerchantFix.ai.

The goal is not to compete on broad ecommerce keywords.

The goal is to capture urgent, high-intent searches from Shopify merchants and ecommerce professionals who are already facing Google Merchant Center errors, Google Shopping product visibility issues, or Shopify product data problems.

## Core SEO principle

MerchantFix.ai must target the exact moment of panic.

The best SEO opportunity is not broad education.

The best SEO opportunity is being visible when a merchant copies a Google Merchant Center error message, a Shopify Google Shopping issue, or a product data warning into Google.

## Positioning

MerchantFix.ai should not try to rank first on broad keywords such as:

Google Shopping optimization.

Product feed optimization.

Ecommerce marketing.

Google Ads ecommerce.

Feed management software.

These keywords are too broad, too competitive, and often dominated by large agencies, established SaaS tools, Google documentation, and long-standing ecommerce blogs.

MerchantFix.ai should target narrow, urgent, problem-specific queries.

Examples:

Missing GTIN Google Merchant Center.

Missing MPN Google Merchant Center.

Incorrect identifier_exists Shopify.

Google Merchant Center product disapproved Shopify.

Google Shopping products not showing Shopify.

Google Merchant Center GTIN MPN error.

Shopify Google Merchant Center checker.

Shopify Google Shopping audit.

Shopify product image issues Google Shopping.

Google Merchant Center price mismatch.

Google Merchant Center availability mismatch.

These searches come from users who already have a problem.

## SEO category

MerchantFix.ai should focus on SEO of urgency.

This means the pages must answer immediate, specific, technical problems.

The target user is not casually learning.

The target user is trying to fix a blocked, limited, rejected, or suspicious product feed.

The page must solve the problem quickly, clearly, and safely.

## SEO strategy name

This strategy is called Sniper SEO.

The strategy is to target exact error phrases and narrow long-tail searches where large competitors are too generic or too slow.

MerchantFix.ai should publish pages that act like emergency repair guides.

Each page must be precise.

Each page must match one error or one tightly related family of errors.

Each page must offer a manual fix first and then show how MerchantFix.ai can help.

## Why this strategy fits MerchantFix.ai

MerchantFix.ai is built for a specific crisis.

The user sees a Google Merchant Center error.

The user does not fully understand the error.

The user wants to know what to fix.

The user wants to avoid making the problem worse.

The user may be willing to pay if the manual process is slow, risky, or confusing.

This fits MerchantFix.ai better than generic ecommerce SEO.

Section 2: short urgent explanation.

Section 3: what the error or issue means.

## Manual fix first strategy

Every SEO page should provide the manual fix.

This builds trust.

The page should explain what the merchant can do without MerchantFix.ai.

Then MerchantFix.ai becomes the faster and safer option.

The logic is:

Show the problem.

Show the manual path.

Show that the manual path is tedious or risky.

Offer MerchantFix.ai as a faster diagnostic and safe correction tool.

## Manual fix example for missing GTIN

A missing GTIN page should explain:

Check whether the product has a real GTIN.

Check the product packaging.

Check manufacturer product data.

Check whether Shopify Variant Barcode is filled.

Check whether the product is custom or handmade.

Do not invent a GTIN.

Do not use SKU as GTIN.

If the product has no manufacturer identifier, review identifier_exists logic.

Use MerchantFix.ai to identify affected rows and separate safe fixes from manual review.

## Manual fix example for V0.5 Shopify surface scan pages

A Shopify Google Shopping audit page should explain:

Check whether product pages have visible images.

Check whether products show clear prices.

Check whether product titles are specific enough.

Check whether descriptions are empty or too weak.

Check whether public product data is accessible.

Upload a Shopify CSV for deeper identifier checks.

Do not assume visible public data equals Google Merchant Center feed data.

## Community answer example

If Google Merchant Center is showing missing GTIN or MPN, first check whether the products actually have manufacturer identifiers. For branded products, Google may expect real GTIN or MPN data. For custom, handmade, or personalized products, you may need to review identifier_exists instead. Do not invent GTINs and do not use SKU as GTIN. MerchantFix.ai can scan a Shopify CSV to find affected rows and mark what can be safely corrected versus what needs manual review.

## New error response process

When a new repeated error appears:

Collect the exact error wording.

Check whether it is feed-level, product-level, website-level, or account-level.

Decide whether it belongs to current scope.

If current scope, create or update a page.

If future scope, add it to backlog.

If risky, publish checklist only.

Never promise automatic fix unless the engine truly supports it.
