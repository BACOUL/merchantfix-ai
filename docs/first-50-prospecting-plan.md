# MerchantFix.ai — First 50 Prospecting Plan

Status: preparation plan for controlled sales.

Date: 2026-06-28

## Purpose

Prepare a small, high-quality prospect list before Vercel launch access returns.

The goal is not mass outreach.

The goal is to identify 20 to 50 good-fit prospects who are likely to understand a 29 EUR Shopify CSV diagnostic for Google Merchant Center product-data warnings.

## Target outcome

Before launch QA is complete, prepare:

```text
20 to 50 prospects
```

After launch QA is Green or acceptable Yellow, contact them in small batches.

Initial target:

```text
3 to 10 controlled Fix Pack sales
```

## Prospect types

### Priority 1 — Shopify agencies

Why they matter:

- they manage multiple Shopify stores;
- they often see Merchant Center issues repeatedly;
- one good agency can become repeat demand;
- they understand CSV/product feeds better than most merchants.

Good signs:

- Shopify service page;
- Google Shopping or Google Ads service page;
- ecommerce migration/service page;
- feed optimization mention;
- product catalog work;
- case studies with retail/ecommerce clients.

### Priority 2 — Google Ads freelancers and consultants

Why they matter:

- they are close to Merchant Center warnings;
- they need practical fixes for clients;
- they may not want to manually inspect CSV rows.

Good signs:

- Google Shopping campaigns;
- ecommerce ads;
- Merchant Center support;
- product feed optimization;
- Shopify client examples.

### Priority 3 — Shopify merchants

Why they matter:

- they feel the pain directly;
- they may pay quickly if the warning blocks sales.

Good signs:

- active Shopify store;
- visible Google Shopping activity;
- public posts asking about Merchant Center warnings;
- store has many products;
- product identifiers, images, prices, or variants are likely complex.

## Avoid these prospects first

Avoid early outreach to:

- non-Shopify stores;
- marketplaces only;
- very small one-product stores;
- merchants asking only about account-level policy issues;
- people expecting guaranteed outcomes;
- people looking for a full feed-management app;
- enterprise prospects requiring procurement/security review.

| Signal | Points |
| --- | --- |

## Research rules

Do:

- collect only business-relevant public information;
- prefer official websites, LinkedIn profiles, agency pages, or public posts;
- avoid private/personal data unrelated to business contact;
- do not scrape aggressively;
- do not spam;
- contact in small batches;
- record why the prospect is relevant.

Do not:

- buy large cold email lists;
- contact thousands of people before QA;
- claim partnership with Google or Shopify;
- promise approval, traffic, rankings, or sales;
- present the tool as a full feed-management platform.

## Contact batch strategy

After launch QA passes:

### Batch 1

```text
5 prospects
```

Goal:

- test message clarity;
- see if people understand the offer;
- collect first warning examples.

### Batch 2

```text
10 prospects
```

Goal:

- test agency/freelancer angle;
- look for repeat-use interest.

### Batch 3

```text
15 to 35 prospects
```

Goal:

- find first 3 to 10 paid tests;
- identify recurring objections.

## Outreach message — agency/freelancer

```text
Hi,

I’m testing MerchantFix.ai, a diagnostic tool for Shopify merchants with Google Merchant Center product-data warnings.

The merchant pastes the warning, uploads a Shopify product CSV, and receives a row-level report showing what to fix, what needs proof, and what not to change yet.

I’m looking for a few real cases for a controlled 29 EUR Fix Pack test.

Do you currently have a Shopify client with product-data warnings in Merchant Center?
```

## Outreach message — merchant

```text
Hi,

If your Shopify products are blocked or limited in Google Merchant Center because of product-data warnings, I’m testing MerchantFix.ai.

You paste the exact warning, upload your Shopify product CSV, and receive a row-level report showing which products to review, what evidence is needed, and what not to change yet.

The first controlled Fix Pack is 29 EUR.

Do you have the exact warning text from Merchant Center?
```

## First reply filter

If someone replies, ask:

```text
1. Is the store on Shopify?
2. Can you export a Shopify product CSV?
3. Is the issue about product data?
4. Are you looking for row-level guidance before editing Shopify?
```

Only send the paid link if the fit is clear.

## Objections to track

Track these carefully:

- price too high;
- price too low / trust concern;
- wants a free diagnosis first;
- wants guaranteed approval;
- cannot export Shopify CSV;
- issue is not product-data related;
- wants agency dashboard;
- wants PDF or email report;
- wants direct feed app integration;
- asks whether this replaces Google support.

## Success signals

Strong signal:

- agencies understand the product in one message;
- prospects send real warning text;
- 3+ customers pay after minimal explanation;
- customers can export CSV;
- report output clarifies what to review;
- agencies ask about repeat use.

Weak signal:

- prospects do not understand the offer;
- most cases are outside product-data diagnosis;
- people require guaranteed outcomes;
- CSV export is a major blocker;
- nobody wants to pay even 29 EUR.

## Stop outreach if

Stop outreach if:

- product QA is not Green or acceptable Yellow;
- checkout fails;
- diagnostic access fails;
- reports are confusing;
- customers misunderstand the tool as guaranteed approval;
- support burden becomes too high.

## Decision after first outreach batch

After the first 5 prospects, decide:

```text
Message understood? yes/no
Warning examples collected? yes/no
Good-fit prospects exist? yes/no
Need to change positioning? yes/no
```

After 20 prospects, decide:

```text
Continue same target
Focus agencies only
Focus freelancers only
Improve public page explanation
Delay sales and fix onboarding
```

## Final rule

Prospecting starts only after product QA passes.

Before Vercel returns, prepare the list.

After Vercel returns, test the product first, then contact prospects in small batches.
