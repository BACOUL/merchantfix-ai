# MerchantFix.ai Revenue Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the future revenue strategy for MerchantFix.ai.

It must not change the V1 product scope.

MerchantFix.ai V1 remains focused on product validation, CSV diagnosis, report clarity, safe corrections, and technical reliability.

Paid offers start only after V1 diagnostic reliability is validated.

---

## Core revenue principles

- No paid offer before V1 diagnostic reliability is validated.
- V1 must remain focused on free or controlled product validation.
- Paid offers start in V2 only.
- Stripe must not be added in V1.
- Subscriptions must not be added in V1.
- Agency plans must not be added in V1.
- MerchantFix.ai must never guarantee Google Merchant Center approval.
- MerchantFix.ai must never invent a GTIN.
- MerchantFix.ai must never invent an MPN.
- MerchantFix.ai must never invent a brand.
- MerchantFix.ai must never apply aggressive CSV corrections without traceability.
- Automatic corrections must be clearly separated from manual review recommendations.
- Original customer data must be preserved.
- The user must understand what was changed, what was not changed, and what still requires manual verification.
- Agency plans come only after the one-time paid offer is validated.

---

## Product positioning

MerchantFix.ai is not a low-cost Shopify feed management app.

MerchantFix.ai is a targeted diagnostic and correction tool for Shopify merchants facing Google Merchant Center product data issues.

The product is positioned around urgent and specific problems such as:

- missing GTIN;
- invalid GTIN;
- missing MPN;
- missing brand;
- incorrect or inconsistent `identifier_exists`;
- product identifier inconsistencies;
- Shopify CSV product data issues that may affect Google Merchant Center visibility.

MerchantFix.ai should be priced based on the value of quickly diagnosing and correcting a painful Merchant Center issue, not only by comparison with low-cost monthly Shopify feed apps.

## V1 revenue status

V1 is not a paid version.

### V1 goal

The goal of V1 is to validate that MerchantFix.ai can reliably:

- parse Shopify CSV files;
- normalize Shopify CSV columns;
- detect identifier-related issues;
- classify issues by severity;
- explain issues clearly;
- recommend safe corrections;
- generate a corrected CSV only when the correction is deterministic and safe;
- avoid invented product identifiers;
- preserve original data;
- display clear product limitations.

## Go / no-go criteria before enabling paid offers

Paid offers must not be enabled until the following conditions are met.

### Product reliability

- V1 diagnostic works on several Shopify CSV samples.
- CSV parsing is stable.
- Column normalization is reliable.
- Identifier issue detection is accurate enough for a paid diagnostic.
- The report clearly separates critical issues, warnings, and informational notes.
- The corrected CSV does not invent GTIN, MPN, or brand values.
- The corrected CSV preserves original customer data.
- The user can understand what was changed.

### Legal and trust

- No guarantee of Google Merchant Center approval is displayed.
- Product limitations are visible.
- Privacy wording is clear.
- Data handling is explained.
- Terms are published.
- Refund/support process is defined.
- Manual review limits are explained if applicable.

### User experience

- Upload flow is understandable.
- Error states are clear.
- Report page is readable.
- Mobile and desktop layouts are usable.
- Example report is available.
- Pricing is transparent.

### QA

- Basic QA checklist is passed.
- Sample CSV tests are passed.
- Edge cases are tested.
- Large files are tested within reasonable V1 limits.
- Broken CSV files are handled cleanly.
- Empty files are handled cleanly.
- Unsupported files are rejected clearly.

## Refund and support principles

Before paid offers are enabled, MerchantFix.ai must define:

- when a refund is available;
- what happens if no automatic correction is possible;
- how users contact support;
- what delivery means for each offer;
- what is not included;
- whether manual review is included or excluded;
- how long the customer can access the result;
- whether uploaded CSV files are stored or not.

The refund policy must be simple and visible before payment.

The long-term revenue model should combine:
